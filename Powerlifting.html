POWERLIFTING

Powerlifting is a strength sport that consists of three attempts at maximal weight on three lifts: squat, bench press, and deadlift. As in the sport of Olympic weightlifting, it involves the athlete attempting a maximal weight single-lift effort of a barbell loaded with weight plates.

SQUAT

 Rules

  • You need to bend your knees and lower the body until the upper quad at the hip joint is lower than the top of the knee

  • You need to have your knees locked at both the beginning and end of the movement

  • You can’t step forward or backward, or move your feet laterally while squatting

  • You must listen to the referee's signals of "squat" and "rack"

  • You can’t touch your elbows or arms with your legs

  • Once on the upwards phase of the movement, the bar cannot move downwards

 
  Equipment

The mandatory equipment in IPF competitions are: a singlet (one piece lifting suit), shoes (either flat or squat shoes with a heel are acceptable), and a t-shirt. The optional pieces of equipment are wearing a set of wrist wraps, knee sleeves, and a belt, although most competitive powerlifters choose to wear these.

BENCH PRESS

    
 Rules

  • The bar needs to be lowered to the chest or abdominal area

  • The bar can't move downwards on the upwards phase

  • You must listen to the referee's commands of "start", "press" and "rack"

  • The Elbows must be fully locked out before the start command and after completion of the lift before the rack command

  • The feet must stay flat on the floor

  • The bottom, head and shoulders must stay intact with the bench throughout the entire lift

 
  Equipment

The mandatory equipment is: a singlet, shoes (either heeled or flat), and a t-shirt. The optional pieces of equipment are wearing a set of wrist wraps and a belt.

DEADLIFT

 Rules

  • You need to lift the barbell off the floor until you are stood upright with your hips and shoulders locked out

  • You must listen to the referees signals of "down"

  • You must lower the bar to the floor without dropping it

  • The bar cannot rest or be supported by the thighs at any stage in the lift

  • The bar cannot move downwards at any stage before the down command

 
  Equipment

The mandatory equipment is: a singlet, shoes(usually flat with a thin sole), knee length socks, and a t-shirt. The optional pieces of equipment are wearing a belt, which most competitive powerlifters choose to wear.

SCORING

The combined total of each lifter's highest completed lift from the squat, bench and deadlift determines their total. However, in order to compare between sexes and different weight classes, the IPF uses Good Lift Points.
